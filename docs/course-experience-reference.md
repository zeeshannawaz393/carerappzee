# How UK care e-learning courses are really taken — reference

> A field reference for how care workers actually take online mandatory/CPD courses on the leading UK
> platforms: the **learner journey**, **course anatomy** (what is genuinely inside a course), **screen-by-
> screen progression**, **assessment**, **certification**, and the **assignment → due-date → reminder →
> renewal → compliance** lifecycle. Purpose: to serve as the spec for building a *realistic* in-app course
> experience (our current learning centre is a summary-card + short quiz; this documents what the real thing
> looks like). Compiled 2026-07-02 from multi-source research; providers are precise about catalogue/admin
> features but thin on second-by-second mechanics, so items are marked **[CITED]** or **[INFERRED]** in the
> tables. Providers surveyed: **iHASCO** (Atlas LMS), **Social Care TV**, **MyLearningCloud** (Lumis LMS),
> **Careskills Academy**, **Grey Matter/Flourish "Click"**, **Access Learning** (LMS Evo), **NHS e-LfH / ESR**.

## 1. The end-to-end learner journey (happy path)

1. **Email invite → one-click / password login** into a personal **learner dashboard** in a web portal
   (iHASCO **Atlas**, Click, MyLearningCloud **Lumis**, Access **LMS Evo**), usually with a **mobile app** too
   (iHASCO Atlas, Click iOS, Access mobile learning app).
2. **"My Learning" list** of enrolled/mandatory courses, each a **card with a status** and a **due date**;
   iHASCO lets the learner **sort by "Due"** to prioritise deadlines.
3. **Launch** a course → an **intro / learning-objectives** screen → the **first module**.
4. Work through **modules/sections** — **video-led** (iHASCO, Social Care TV) or **interactive slides with
   scenarios** (MyLearningCloud, Careskills, Click, Access) — advancing **screen-by-screen** with a
   **Next/Continue** button and a **progress indicator**.
5. **A knowledge check after each module** (auto-marked MCQ, instant feedback) — *ungraded*, to reinforce.
6. **Stop and resume any time** — the LMS **bookmarks where you left off** (SCORM) and reopens at that screen;
   a course can be done in one sitting or over days/weeks.
7. **A final graded assessment** decides completion — MCQ, a **pass mark**, **multiple attempts**.
8. On pass → an **auto-issued PDF certificate** (name, date, score, expiry, CPD/accreditation).
9. The completion flows to the manager's **compliance matrix**; an **expiry/renewal** date is set; **refresher
   reminders** fire before it lapses.

## 2. Course anatomy — what is actually inside a course

Four structural styles dominate:

| Provider | Structure | Typical size | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| **iHASCO** | A few named **"sections"** + a final test | **3–5 sections**, 30–60 min | Interactive slides, video-led, subtitles/auto-translate |
| **Social Care TV** | Many fine-grained **"modules"**, MCQ after each | **10–26 modules**, 35–100 min | **Audio-visual (video)** demonstrations |
| **MyLearningCloud** | Named **"units"** (explicit "Number of Units: X"), any order | **2–4 units**, 20 min–3 hr | Interactive e-learning |
| **NHS e-LfH** | Single linear **"session"** | **1 session**, ~20 min | Interactive, scenarios + knowledge checks |

### Real module breakdowns (from provider course-detail pages)

**Care Certificate — 16 standards (2025; iHASCO ships 16, some still ship 15).** Delivered as 16 sections
(iHASCO, ~300 min / 5 hr) or 20 topic-courses (Social Care TV, 15–18 hr) or per-standard unit courses
(MyLearningCloud): 1 Understand your role · 2 Your personal development · 3 Duty of care · 4 Equality &
diversity · 5 Work in a person-centred way · 6 Communication · 7 Privacy & dignity · 8 Fluids & nutrition ·
9 Awareness of mental health, dementia & learning disability · 10 Safeguarding adults · 11 Safeguarding
children · 12 Basic life support · 13 Health & safety · 14 Handling information · 15 Infection prevention &
control · **16 Learning disability & autism**.

**Safeguarding Adults (Social Care TV, 21 modules, 65–80 min)** — Introduction · Definition of abuse ·
Definition of neglect · Who abuses? · Responsibilities · Policies & procedures · Risk assessment ·
Safeguarding leads · Legislation · Safeguarding Adult Boards · The six principles · Fundamental standards ·
Code of conduct · Indicators of abuse · Physical / domestic / sexual / psychological / financial abuse ·
Modern slavery · Discriminatory & organisational abuse · Neglect & self-neglect · What to do on
disclosure/suspicion · Recording concerns · Information sharing · Whistleblowing. (Sector convention:
Level 1 = awareness all staff; Level 2 = direct work with adults at risk; Level 3 = safeguarding leads.)

**Moving & Handling of People (Social Care TV, 10 modules, 35–45 min; video demos)** — back pain & injury ·
cumulative injury · work as trained · prevention · how your back works · anatomy (spine/discs/ligaments) ·
ergonomics · movements & bearing weight · safe-handling principles & correct position · infection control &
promoting independence. (MyLearningCloud = **3 units, any order**; Careskills "Lite" = **4 modules, Level 2**.)

**Infection Prevention & Control (iHASCO 3 sections, 35 min = Infections / Chain of infection / Hygiene;
MyLearningCloud 4 units = What is infection / Preventing the spread / Responsibilities / COVID-19, 70 min).**

**Medication (iHASCO "Medication Awareness for Care", 3 sections, 30 min)** — Policies & the law · Medication ·
Administering medication. (Careskills runs a tiered ladder: Lite → Practice → Advanced → L2 qualification.)

**Basic Life Support (e-LfH "Standard 12: BLS Level 1", single session ~20 min)** — CPR, choking, cardiac
arrest, chest compressions, calling 999. (Social Care TV "First Aid Awareness" = 26 modules, 75–90 min,
including DRABC/AVPU/recovery position/CPR/defibrillators.)

**Fire Safety — care (iHASCO 5 sections, 35 min)** — Fire in care settings · Signs · Fire hazards ·
Emergency procedure · Fire extinguishers. (Fire Warden variant adds RRFSO 2005, risk assessments, PEEPs.)

**Mental Capacity Act (iHASCO "Assessing Mental Capacity", 5 sections, 40 min)** — The MCA 2005 · The five
principles · Helping people make decisions · Capacity assessment · Disagreements & complaints. **DoLS is a
separate 30-min course.** (Social Care TV "Mental Capacity" = 25 modules, 50–60 min.)

**Equality, Diversity & Inclusion (iHASCO, ~1 hr, 5 sections)** — meanings of EDI · barriers · protected
characteristics & the Equality Act · practical steps · embracing difference (updated for gender identity,
allyship, equity).

**Food Safety (Social Care TV, ~25 modules, 80–100 min)** — legislation, HACCP, personal hygiene, CoSHH,
the 14 allergens, contamination types, food poisoning pathogens, the 4 C's, cross-contamination, the danger
zone, cooking/chilling/storage temperatures, use-by dates.

> **CPD note:** providers rarely print a CPD *number* — they say "CPD Accredited/Certified" and give a
> **run-time in minutes**; treat run-time as the CPD-time proxy.

## 3. Screen-by-screen progression & interaction types

- **Navigation:** screen-by-screen with a **Next / Continue** button (baseline), a **menu/sidebar** of
  sections, and a **progress bar** (% or "3 of 8"). Courses built in Articulate **Storyline/Rise 360** (which
  most vendors use) get these out of the box.
- **In-screen interaction types** used in care courses: rich **text**, **video** (with captions/transcript),
  **click-to-reveal / tabs / accordions / flip cards** (defining terms, signs of abuse, types of dementia),
  **labeled graphics / hotspots** (anatomy, hazard-spotting, PPE), **drag-and-drop / sorting** (matching
  duties, safeguarding categories, waste segregation), **branching "what would you do" scenarios** with
  consequences (safeguarding, consent, challenging behaviour), **reflective prompts**, and **timeline/process**
  interactions (care pathways, incident-reporting steps).
- **Knowledge checks (per module):** *ungraded* check-ins, **auto-marked**, formats = single MCQ, multi-select,
  true/false, matching, fill-in-the-blank; **feedback on right/wrong**; retries can be limited; distinct from
  the graded final test.

## 4. Gating, progress & resume

**Two gating philosophies:**
- **Lock the video / no-skip** — iHASCO's optional **"Mandatory Videos"** setting **locks the timeline slider
  so learners cannot fast-forward**; in Storyline the equivalent is **disabling Next until the slide timeline
  completes** (a per-slide minimum dwell), plus **Restricted** (no jumping ahead) or **Locked** (strict order)
  menu modes. *Notably, iHASCO itself advises against locking and prefers raising the pass mark.*
- **Free scrub + stiff end-test** — Social Care TV lets learners **rewind/pause/replay freely** and enforces
  engagement via the **final assessment** rather than gating.

**Video controls:** play/pause, timeline scrub (unless locked), volume, **captions with auto-translate**,
time remaining, fullscreen, in-video markers revealing extra content.

**Resume / bookmarking (SCORM run-time — this is the engineering core):**
- `cmi.location` — a free-text **bookmark of the current slide/lesson** (255 chars in 1.2, 1000 in 2004).
- `cmi.suspend_data` — persistent **granular state** (answers, blocks completed). **Cap: 4 KB (SCORM 1.2) /
  64 KB (SCORM 2004)** — the main constraint (vendors compress it).
- `cmi.entry` = `ab-initio` vs `resume` → drives the "start over / continue where you left off" prompt.
- `cmi.exit` = `suspend` on mid-course exit so the attempt is preserved.
- `cmi.completion_status` (`completed/incomplete/…`) + `cmi.progress_measure` (0–1) + `cmi.score.scaled`.
- Flow: on each step the content `SetValue`s `location` + `suspend_data`; on exit sets `exit=suspend` and
  `Commit`s; on relaunch the LMS returns `entry=resume` and the content jumps straight to the saved screen.
  (xAPI equivalent: resume via the **State API Activity State document**; progress via statements; no 4 KB cap.)

## 5. Final assessment (graded — separate from knowledge checks)

| Provider | Pass mark | Attempts | Randomised | Review answers | # Questions |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Careskills Academy** | **80%** | Unlimited "Retake assessment" | **Yes** (never the same set twice) | **Yes** (dropdown under score) | shown in-app |
| **iHASCO** | **Default 60%** (admin-raisable) | Admin result-action (retake test / mark) | not documented | re-take test or re-watch sections | **varies** (e.g. 5–20) |
| **Social Care TV** | **Employer-set** — cert shows **% not pass/fail** | practice free; fresh certified attempt = **1 credit** | — | — | not published |
| **Rise 360 default** (authoring engine many use) | **80%** | unlimited by default (cappable) | **question-bank draw** | show correct / incorrect+feedback / hide | author-set |

**Nobody does "one shot."** Retake is normally the **test only**, not the whole course. iHASCO Care Certificate
final test = **20 questions**.

## 6. Certificate & CPD

**Auto-issued PDF on pass is universal.** iHASCO's is the most explicit: **name, company, course, pass %,
completion date, expiry date, accreditation stamps**, reprintable from Atlas. Social Care TV auto-generates
and **emails it in seconds**, showing a **% (no pass/fail)**. **Validity is typically ~1 year** with a
refresher prompt; iHASCO/Access set it via a per-course **renewal interval / expiry date**.

## 7. Assignment → due-date → reminder → renewal → compliance

- **Assignment / enrolment:** manual bulk-enrol **plus auto-rules** — iHASCO **Segments + Smart Enrolments**,
  Access LMS Evo **dynamic rules + category tagging**, MyLearningCloud **role-based pathways**, Click assign
  from the matrix. NHS = self-enrol content but **ESR Learning Certifications/competencies** drive assignment.
- **Due dates:** set **per assignment/deadline**; the learner sees courses **flagged by due date and sortable**
  (iHASCO "sort by Due"). ESR sets a **"next Due Date"**.
- **Reminders & overdue:** iHASCO **daily** (new enrolment / expiring / required reading) + **weekly Monday**
  summaries (not-started / in-progress / due-within-a-month / overdue) + a **refresher reminder ~1 month before
  expiry**; Access **configurable frequency, group-targeted, unsociable-hours limit**; MyLearningCloud **weekly
  compliance reminders**. (Fixed 30/60/90-day tiers and manager-escalation chase emails were **not** documented
  — reminders are learner-directed.)
- **Learner dashboard statuses:** **Not started · In progress (with % bar) · Passed · Failed · Expiring soon ·
  Expired**. Access LMS Evo shows a **RAG "My Mandatory Training"**: **Red = Overdue**, **Amber = Due soon
  (default 7-day window, configurable)**, **Green = In date**, with a per-category RAG dial.
- **Renewal / refresher:** iHASCO **"Training Interval" (months) → computes the expiry date**; "New sessions" /
  "Start new session" forces renewal; ESR **auto-renews** renewable certifications. Silent auto-re-enrolment on
  expiry is not confirmed anywhere — the norm is **expiry + refresher reminder + admin/self re-run**.
- **Compliance matrix (manager view):** a **colour-coded matrix** (compliant / due soon / expired / overdue),
  completion %, **Excel/CQC-ready exports** — Flourish, Careskills ("auto colour-coded, real-time, CQC tool"),
  MyLearningCloud (Compliance Matrix + 54 reports), Access (org-wide compliance view).
- **NHS e-LfH / ESR:** content from e-LfH, but **assignment/due-dates/renewal live in ESR**; completions
  **update ESR overnight** and **follow the worker** between NHS employers (unlike the closed commercial LMSs).

## 8. The Care Certificate (blended) & Oliver McGowan — not finishable at a keyboard

- **Care Certificate** = e-learning delivers the **theory/knowledge only**; the certificate needs a
  **workbook (Practice Assessment Document / PAD) + observed practice on shift + assessor sign-off** (senior
  carer / deputy / nurse), over **~8–12 weeks** of induction. A common CQC finding is **lost workbooks /
  incomplete sign-offs**. e-LfH sessions run ~20–30 min each, 24/7, dip-in/dip-out.
- **Oliver McGowan (statutory LD & autism)** = **90-min e-learning (mandatory first) → a live interactive
  session** — **Tier 1** = 1-hour online session **co-led by a person with a learning disability and an
  autistic person**; **Tier 2** = a full **face-to-face day**.
- **CSTF statutory set** (11 subjects) on e-LfH is assessed at **80% pass / 3 attempts**, with **feedback on
  wrong answers** and a **sanctioned "skip straight to the eAssessment"** path.

## 9. Candid reality (how it actually goes)

Much mandatory e-learning is done **at home, in the worker's own time, often unpaid** (against ACAS/minimum-
wage guidance). It is widely experienced as **repetitive, "click-next / death by e-learning."** Because the
official route **permits skipping to the assessment** (80% / 3 attempts / feedback on wrong answers),
**skimming and going for the test is a common, low-risk pattern.** A full CSTF + Care Certificate + Oliver
McGowan stack is roughly **~29 hours** (an estimate, not a firmly cited figure).

## 10. Implications for a realistic in-app build (ours vs the real thing)

| Real-course feature | Our learning centre today | To make it realistic |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-section course, **Next/Continue + progress bar** | ❌ one scrollable lesson | Break a course into **sections**; a **"3 of 8" progress bar**; Next between sections |
| **Video-led** delivery | 🟡 simulated player (toast) | Keep the simulated player but make it the **spine** of a section; a **"watched" gate** option |
| **Gating / no-skip** before assessment | ❌ assessment always open | Optional **lock: complete all sections before the assessment** (the iHASCO/Storyline model) |
| **Per-module knowledge checks** | 🟡 scenarios only | A **check after each section** (ungraded, feedback) + the graded final |
| **Graded final** (pass mark, retry, review answers) | ✅ 80% + retry + **now shows answers** | already matches Careskills |
| **Resume where you left off** | ❌ complete/not only | Persist **current section index** (our `carerStore`) = the SCORM `cmi.location` analog |
| **Assigned + due date + status** | 🟡 condition-JIT only | Add **assigned/mandatory + "Due in X days / Overdue"** flags (RAG) on course cards |
| **Reminders / refresher** | 🟡 refresh chips | Reuse `validityMonths` → expiry; add "renew before …" nudges |
| **Certificate + CPD record** | ✅ built | already matches |
| **Blended Care Cert** (theory + observed sign-off) | 🟡 caveat + Skills observed track | already partially models the blend |

**Biggest gaps to close for realism:** (1) a **multi-section course player with a progress bar**, (2) **resume**
(persist the current section), (3) **assigned + due-date/overdue** framing, and (4) **per-section knowledge
checks**. The video is simulated by design (no real media in the prototype).
